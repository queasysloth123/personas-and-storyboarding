Sektch & Description

Snackpass's kiosk offers a simple touch-screen interface for customers to place their orders without having to wait in line and talk to a cashier.

Key Observations

  • Customers often took a total of 3 minutes to place their order.
  • Users are usually young adults or families.
  • People generally use contactless payment.
  • Order does not always go through.
  • Kiosk has no line.

Interview Summary

Questions asked

  1. Why do you use the self-severing kiosk?
  2. Have you had any difficulties using the kiosk? If so, please describe them.
  3. Do you have the Snackpass mobile app? If so, why do you use the kiosk instead?
  4. How does it feel to place your order without a cashier?
  5. How do you feel about the layout of the kiosk?

Responses

  • Customers often prefer ordering on the Snackpass kiosk because there is no line, no pressure, and it is easy to navigate.
  • Some downsides to using the kiosk include fast order timeouts, transaction failures, and the inability to earn Snackpass points.
  • Users have heard from friends that the kiosk offers discounts if mobile phone number is entered.
  • People feel like they are missing the social interaction that comes with ordering from a cashier.
  • The menu items are too big, which can make scrolling though all the options overwhelming.

Empathy Maps

Amy

Amy is a college student who likes to try new things. She decides to try boba for the first time and orders with Snackpass's kiosk. She proceeds to look through all the menu items, but the icons are too big and take up a lot of space, making the menu feel more overwhelming than it actually is. She also has dietary restrictions and wishes for a way to filter the menu or at least see common allergenic ingredients in each menu item before proceeding to select toppings/sides. Because Amy is taking to long to choose her order, the kiosk times out and terminates her session. Amy tries again, and ends up getting brown sugar boba with soy milk.

Joe

Joe is a college student who doesn't like to try new things. He decides to get boba again for the 2nd time today before going to his next class. He sees the long line to the cashier and becomes worried that he won't get his drink in time. He, instead, orders on Snackpass's kiosk, selecting the first item on the menu. He pays for his drink, and is offered a discount if he enters his phone number. He is disappointed that he link it to his Snackpass account because he is one gift away from hatching an egg with his friend. He enters his number, but his order fails to go through. Joe is upset, tries again, and successfully orders his drink.
